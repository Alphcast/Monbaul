About the Institute

Rooted in tradition, devoted to learning

Monbaul Anwaar — meaning "a source of lights" — is an Islamic institute dedicated to raising students who carry the Qur'an in their hearts and the classical sciences in their minds. Our teachers hold authenticated chains of transmission (isnad) and guide each student with patience and discipline.

Beyond memorisation, students are grounded in the tools needed to understand Islam correctly: the rules of recitation, sound creed, jurisprudence, and Arabic grammar — the same curriculum that has formed scholars for centuries.

  • Qualified, ijazah-holding teachers
  • Structured Hifz memorisation track
  • Separate classes by age and level
  • Strong emphasis on character (akhlaq)
What We Teach

Seven disciplines, one tradition

Each subject builds upon the last, forming a complete and time-tested path of Islamic learning.

قُرْآن

Qur'an Recitation

Correct, fluent reading of the Qur'an from the first letters through to complete mastery of the text.

حِفْظ

Hifz (Memorisation)

Structured memorisation of the full Qur'an with daily revision and regular assessment.

تَجْوِيد

Ahkam at-Tajweed

The precise rules governing pronunciation, articulation points (makharij), and recitation etiquette.

تَوْحِيد

Tawheed

The foundational study of Islamic creed and the oneness of Allah, drawn from Qur'an and authentic Sunnah.

فِقْه

Fiqh

Islamic jurisprudence covering worship, transactions, and daily conduct according to classical scholarship.

نَحْو

Nahw

Arabic syntax and grammar — the structural key to understanding the Qur'an and classical texts directly.

صَرْف

Sarf

Arabic morphology, the study of word patterns and roots that underpins fluency in the classical language.
